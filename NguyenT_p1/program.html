ABOUT

Yale College, the undergraduate division of Yale University, offers a Bachelor of Arts degree program with a major in art.

Yale College, the undergraduate division of Yale University, offers a Bachelor of Arts degree program with a major in art. Students have the unique opportunity to engage deeply with the arts through elective courses and the art major. These programs allow undergraduates to explore various artistic disciplines, build foundational skills, and integrate their creative practice with a broad liberal arts education.

Undergraduate applicants wishing to major in art at Yale must apply to Yale College directly. 

The program in art offers courses that, through work in a variety of media, provide an experience in the visual arts as part of a liberal education as well as preparation for graduate study and professional work.

Undergraduates are normally limited to credit for four terms of graduate or professional-level courses (courses numbered 500 and above).

  • Courses at the 100 level stress the fundamental aspects of visual formulation and articulation.
  • Courses numbered 200 through 499 offer increasingly intensive study leading to greater specialization in one or more of the visual disciplines such as graphic design, painting/printmaking, photography, and sculpture.

    • PREREQUIsiTES

    The prerequisites for acceptance into the major are a Sophomore Review, which is an evaluation of work from studio courses taken at Yale School of Art, and five terms of introductory (100-level) courses. Four must be completed at the time of the Sophomore Review. Visual Thinking (Art 111a or b) and Basic Drawing (Art 114a or b) are mandatory.

    REQUIRED COURSES + -

    Undergraduates pursuing an art major at Yale College must complete a combination of studio courses and art history courses to ensure a well-rounded education. Below is an outline of the required courses:

    Foundation Courses:

    1.ART 111: Basic Drawing

  • An introduction to the principles of drawing, focusing on observation, composition, and various drawing techniques.

    • 2.ART 114: Basic Graphic Design

  • A course that covers the fundamentals of graphic design, including typography, layout, and visual communication.

    • Intermediate Courses:

    1.ART 130: Painting Basics

  • Techniques and concepts of painting with a focus on color theory, composition, and various painting mediums.

    • 2.ART 132: Sculpture Basics

  • Introduction to three-dimensional art forms, exploring materials such as clay, wood, metal, and mixed media.

    • Advanced Courses:

    1.ART 324: Advanced Photography

  • Advanced study of photographic techniques, digital imaging, and the role of photography in contemporary art.

    • 2.ART 426: Advanced Printmaking

  • Exploration of complex printmaking techniques, including etching, lithography, and screen printing.

    • Art History Courses:

    1.ARTH 110: Introduction to Art History

  • A survey of major works and movements in art history from antiquity to the modern era.

    • 2.ARTH 210: Modern and Contemporary Art

  • Study of art from the late 19th century to the present, focusing on key artists, movements, and theoretical approaches.

    HOW TO APPLY + -

    Yale College students interested in pursuing the art major or taking elective courses in the School of Art should follow these steps:

    1.Explore the Curriculum:

  • Review the course offerings and major requirements on the Yale School of Art and Yale College websites.

    • 2.Advising and Consultation:

  • Schedule a meeting with an academic advisor or faculty member in the art department to discuss your interests and academic goals.

    • 3.Declare the Major:

  • If you decide to major in art, formally declare your major through the Yale College registrar's office and complete any necessary paperwork.

    • 4.Submit a Portfolio:

  • While not always required for elective courses, students declaring an art major or applying for advanced studio courses may need to submit a portfolio of their work. Check specific course requirements for details.

    • 5.Enrollment:

  • Register for the desired courses through Yale's online course registration system during the designated enrollment period.

    • 6.Prepare for Class:

  • Obtain any required materials and review the course syllabus to be ready for the first day of class.

    FAQS + -

    1. What programs does the Yale School of Art offer?

  • The Yale School of Art offers a range of graduate programs including MFA degrees in Painting/Printmaking, Sculpture, Photography, and Graphic Design. Undergraduates at Yale College can engage with the School of Art through elective courses and the art major.

    • 2. How do I apply to the Yale School of Art?

  • For graduate programs, applicants must submit an online application, a portfolio of their work, letters of recommendation, and a statement of purpose. Specific application requirements and deadlines are detailed on our Admissions page. Undergraduate students interested in art courses or the art major should follow the application procedures outlined by Yale College.

    • 3. What financial aid options are available?

  • The Yale School of Art offers various forms of financial aid including scholarships, grants, and assistantships. We are committed to making our programs accessible to talented artists regardless of financial background. Detailed information is available on our Financial Aid page.

    • 4. Can I visit the Yale School of Art?

  • Yes, prospective students are encouraged to visit. We offer campus tours, open houses, and opportunities to meet with faculty and current students. Visit our Contact page to schedule a visit or learn more about upcoming events.

    • 5. What facilities are available to students?

  • The School of Art boasts state-of-the-art facilities including studios, galleries, and specialized workshops for painting, sculpture, printmaking, photography, and graphic design. Our resources are designed to support a wide range of artistic practices.

    • 6. How can I view student work and exhibitions?

  • Student work is regularly showcased in our on-campus galleries and online. Check our Student Works page for a gallery of recent projects and our News & Events page for information on upcoming exhibitions and showcases.

    • 7. Who are the faculty members?

  • Our faculty comprises renowned artists, scholars, and industry leaders dedicated to mentoring the next generation of artists. Learn more about our faculty's expertise and achievements on the Faculty page.
